01 Market Definition & Sizing

A real base, hiding behind a misleading headline

"Soccer video analysis" is not one market — your product is a single cell in a grid: consumer/amateur, software-only, upload-and-get-team-stats, soccer-first. Size that, not the pro-dominated headline.

The carve-out trap

Headline "sports analytics is $X billion" figures are dominated by pro clubs, broadcasters and betting. The only defensible base is the registered / club cohort — and FIFA's own split shows how steep the drop is.

265MAll players globally (FIFA Big Count, 2006) — do not size against this
226MUnregistered / occasional — not a per-player-analytics buyer
38MRegistered players — the candidate base (still over-inclusive)
22MYouth under-18 (21.5M registered) — USA 3.9M, Germany 2.08M lead
SOMAfter age & seriousness haircut — a small fraction of registered

02 Demand — The Customer

Three people, one purchase, different wants

In youth football the user, buyer and payer split — the kid uses it, the parent pays, the coach influences. Design for the wrong one and you misread the sale.

The cleaner sale is B2B2C through the club: it collapses buyer + payer into one entity and folds parental consent into the existing membership flow.

Jobs-to-be-done — and how forgiving each is of wrong stats

Forgiving (where tech works)

  • Highlights & sharing — clip detection ≫ stat precision; viral; best wedge
  • Individual development — trends matter more than exact counts
  • Team-level tactics — more tractable than per-player

Unforgiving (where tech breaks)

  • Per-player stat tracking — your headline JTBD; least forgiving
  • Recruitment tape — needs near-truth accuracy

Your stated headline JTBD is the least forgiving and most technically exposed. Highlights / development are where demand is strong and the tech is forgiving — a safer first wedge.

03 Supply — Competition & Money

Everyone automated controls the camera

Only StepOut was independently verified here. Every other competitor and all pricing below is Unverified — a structured hypothesis to confirm.

Verified competitor — StepOut

Broad B2B + individual positioning, marquee logos (Real Madrid, Ajax, 60+ teams), raised $1.5M pre-Series A (Rainmatter 2025). 3-0 But: marquee "clients" are accelerator/pilot relationships, and that it accepts phone footage was Refuted 0-3 — even the closest funded analogue is built for broadcast-grade input. The gap you want is below StepOut.

Positioning map

Left → right = easier for the customer to supply footage. Top → bottom = more human labour in the loop. The automated + any-footage + consumer-price corner is empty.

▲ FULLY AUTOMATED HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP ▼ ◀ NEEDS HARDWARE ANY PHONE FOOTAGE ▶ Veo Trace Pixellot Spiideo XbotGo StepOut* ★ TARGET — empty Hudl Assist Manual / spreadsheets
Needs hardware Software-only* Human-in-the-loop Target (empty) *StepOut software-only but needs broadcast-grade footage

Two patterns decide the strategy. Every credible automated competitor is pushed left into "needs their hardware" — because controlling capture is how they make the CV work. And Hudl Assist defeats the difficulty by spending human labour (~$700/team/season), which is exactly why an automated entrant could undercut it on cost.

A clean per-match or per-season software-only price is a genuine open position — cheaper than human tagging, comparable to AI per-player products.

Go-to-market

  • Club / league B2B2C — amortises CAC over a roster, fixes capture, folds in consent. Solves three risks at once.
  • Highlight-sharing virality loop — the growth engine Veo/Trace lean on; forgiving of imperfect stats; drives CAC toward zero.

04 The Enabling Environment

Commodity blocks, uncommodity problem

The building blocks (YOLO, DeepSORT, MediaPipe) are commodity — so the model is no moat. But generic CV doesn't trivially solve sports, so the work is real. The moat is proprietary in-domain data, not the architecture.

Regulation, privacy & IP — first-order

Verified — UK FA

Written parental consent before footage enters the public domain · consent + disclosure for coaching-aid filming · operational "do-not-film" systems (wristbands / check-ins). Attaches to public-domain use & organised filming — not every incidental frame.

Unverified — clear before launch

US COPPA · EU/UK GDPR biometric (special-category) data · Australian Privacy Act · image/likeness rights · data ownership · patent landscape. Your largest segment (youth) carries the heaviest burden.

Macro

Tailwinds

  • Cheaper compute & CV
  • Ubiquitous phone cameras
  • Rising youth-sports spend; recruitment culture
  • Highlight / creator culture · 2026 World Cup

Headwinds

  • Intensifying privacy scrutiny
  • Pressure on discretionary spend
  • Justified AI-accuracy skepticism at consumer prices

06 Unit Economics Deep-Dive Follow-up

Compute is cheap. LTV is the wall.

The real constraint — LTV vs CAC under seasonal churn

Pure B2C per-player LTV is capped near the ~$36 Year-1 fitness ceiling Verified. To hit 3:1 LTV:CAC, CAC must stay under ~$12 — hard for a niche prosumer product. This is LTV-constrained, not cost-constrained. The fix: sell per-team / per-season so one purchase covers a roster, and manage seasonal churn (annual commitment, off-season pause).

The trap that flips everything

If accuracy on phone footage is too low, you're forced into human QA at ~$25–40/match (Hudl-Assist-class labour) — 10–20× the compute cost — which erases the 74–86% margin. The technology gap and the unit-economics risk are the same risk from two angles.

07 Technology Maturity Deep-Dive Follow-up

The product hinges on the join nobody has solved

Detecting what happened from raw video is surprisingly mature. Attributing it to a named individual from one ground-level camera is the unsolved core — and every benchmark below is a broadcast ceiling; amateur phone footage is worse on every axis.

GS-HOTA ~64

Closest proxy for the whole product. ~⅓ of player-frames still wrong on identity + position — on broadcast video, with a lenient 5-metre tolerance. Benchmark only introduced in 2024.

73 mAP@1

Event spotting (T-DEED) — end-to-end from pixels, no tracking input. The good news: detecting what/when doesn't need the broken tracking stack. The gap is purely who.

Verdict — years, not months

A reliable consumer product doing accurate per-player attribution from one amateur phone is plausibly a multi-year (~3–5+ yr) effort, gated by re-ID, jersey legibility and end-to-end attribution under amateur capture. What's achievable now: team-level analytics, heatmaps, and event spotting + highlight clips — exactly the forgiving wedge the verdict points to.

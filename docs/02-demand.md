## 2.1 Separate the user, the buyer and the payer

The defining feature of youth/grassroots sports demand: **the three roles split**, and they often
*want different things*. Designing for "the user" alone misreads who you must convince.

| Segment | User (uses it) | Buyer (chooses it) | Payer (pays) | What each actually wants |
|---------|----------------|--------------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Youth player | The kid | The parent/coach | The parent | Kid: highlights to share. Parent: development proof + safety. Coach: team insight. |
| Adult amateur / rec | The player | The player | The player | Bragging-rights stats, highlight clips, "am I improving?" |
| Grassroots club | Players/coach | Coach/committee | Club budget | One roster-wide tool, easy capture, low admin |
| Semi-pro | Players | Coach/analyst | Club | Tactical + individual *accuracy* (your weak spot, §4) |

**Implication:** for the youth segment — likely your largest by headcount — you are selling to a
**parent who pays for a child who uses it, influenced by a coach.** That is a three-sided sale with a
privacy overlay (§2.4). The cleaner sale is **B2B2C through the club**, which collapses buyer+payer into
one entity and solves capture + consent at once.

## 2.2 Jobs-to-be-done (and how forgiving each is of imperfect stats)

| JTBD | Demand intensity (hypothesis) | Accuracy tolerance | Notes |
|------|-------------------------------|--------------------|-------|
| Highlights & sharing | High, viral | **Very forgiving** | Clip detection ≫ stat precision; best wedge |
| Individual development | High (parents) | Moderate | Trends matter more than exact counts |
| Stat tracking (per-player) | **Your headline** | **Unforgiving** | Wrong stats erode trust fast — and this is the unsolved CV problem (§4) |
| Tactical coaching | Medium | Moderate | Team-level, more tractable than per-player |
| Recruitment tape | High value, low volume | **Unforgiving** | Needs near-truth; not a consumer wedge |
| Live streaming | Medium | N/A | Adjacent; capture-hardware play |

> **Strategic read:** your stated headline JTBD (per-player stat tracking) is the *least* forgiving and
> the *most* technically exposed. The **highlights/development** JTBDs are where demand is strong *and*
> the tech is forgiving — a far safer initial wedge that still produces "good enough" individual stats.

## 2.4 Willingness to pay & adoption barriers

### Willingness to pay `[UNVERIFIED]`
No WTP figures were confirmed by the research. What is *structurally* true:
- WTP **varies enormously by segment** — a parent's discretionary spend per child ≠ a club budget ≠ a
  semi-pro analyst's tool budget.
- Your early SOM math (§1.2) shows the model needs **volume at low price**, so WTP fragility directly
  threatens unit economics. **This must be measured** (fake-door + interviews), not assumed.

### Adoption barriers (the recurring, structural ones)

1. **Footage-capture friction — the #1 barrier.** Someone must film a **wide, elevated, legible** shot.
   This is *exactly* what makes the CV tractable (§4) — and it is why hardware players (Veo, Trace,
   XbotGo) win by *selling the capture solution*. **"Upload any phone footage" maximises this barrier's
   damage:** ground-level, narrow, shaky, partially-occluded video is the worst input for the per-player
   attribution you're promising. `[VERIFIED that pipelines need broadcast-grade input; §4]`
2. **Minors' privacy & consent (UK FA, verified).** `[VERIFIED 3-0]` The FA requires:
   - **written parental consent** before a child's photo/film enters the **public domain** (collected
     at season start via the club membership/consent form);
   - **consent + disclosure** when filming is used as a **coaching aid**, with care in storing clips;
   - clubs/organisers to run **operational systems** (badges/stickers/wristbands/check-ins) so
     photographers can identify children who must **not** be filmed.
   *Scope nuance (verified):* this attaches to **public-domain use and organised filming**, not every
   incidental frame — maximalist claims that *all* capture of under-16s needs prior consent, or that
   *only trained personnel* may film, were **`[REFUTED 0-3]`**. So the friction is **real but narrower**
   than a worst-case reading — and it is **UK-specific**; US/EU/AU rules are unverified (§4, open Q).
3. **Time.** Upload, processing wait, and reviewing output compete with a busy parent/coach's time.
4. **Accuracy skepticism.** If the per-player stats are visibly wrong, trust collapses — and the tech
   (§4) says they *will* be wrong on poor footage. This is a demand barrier *created by* the supply-side
   limitation.
